Rehwa Society Rehwa Society, founded in 1979 by Richard and Sally Holkar in Maheshwar, supports traditional Maheshwari weaving, growing from 12 women to 250 weavers. Profits fund welfare initiatives, preserving culture and livelihoods while showcasing their craft through exhibitions. The organization also invests in education, housing, and healthcare for the weavers, ensuring community well-being.

Gudi Mudi Established in 2003 in Maheshwar, Women Weavers empowers local women by making handloom weaving a dignified livelihood. The Charitable Trust provides skill training and connects weavers with retailers, designers, and customers across India and internationally, promoting sustainable livelihoods and high-quality products. By fostering community development, the trust ensures the art of weaving thrives for future generations.

Old Houses The old houses of Maheshwar, rich in heritage, have been transformed into business hubs for weaving workshops and local crafts. These spaces preserve the town's cultural legacy while supporting sustainable livelihoods for artisans and attracting global customers. Visitors to these workshops not only purchase authentic handwoven products but also experience the vibrant history and craftsmanship that have been passed down through generations.
